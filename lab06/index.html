Site Accessability Review

What is the URL of the website?

Burk Museum

What is the name of the website?

All About Frogs

Who is the site's target audience?

People who are learning about frogs and what makes them unique compared to toads. This paired with other facts and commomly asked questions about frogs gives the audience insight about that creature.

How is the site organized?

The site is a Hierarchical Organization, with the page in question being a subcatagory/page in relation to the home. This page has its contents in a more centered format, meaning all of the information is read down the middle of the page with the text alligned to the left. It makes the page have a cleaner look while also making it easily transferable to a mobile format, while also making it easy to read. The navigation bar is always present when scrolling so the user can access other pages without having to scroll back to the top of the page.

Which "CRAP" Design Principle does the use? Provide at least one example.

The CRAP Design Principle that this site utilizes is the Repitition and Contrast aspect due to the text being centered on the page and the allignment of it shifted to the left. As for the Contrast, the font size of the headings are consistant, along with the smaller font size present in the bodys.

What is the Audit Score according to the Accessibility Checker?

According to the Audit Score on the Accessibility Checker, the site has a score of 17%, with 14 Critical Issues and 34 Not Applicables.

What is the site's effectiveness? Does it support users in completing actions accurately?

When it comes to site effectiveness, it is easy to scroll and read the information, but for navigation, it lacks in being clear. I originally found this site on Google linking to this section of the webpage, but after trying to navigate to the "All About Frogs" section from the home, I was easily lost. It wasn't too clear as to how one can travel from page to page and the only way I got back was through the "Back" part of the browser.

What is the site's efficiency? Can users can perform tasks quickly?

As for performing tasks, it's easy top switch to a different page since the navagation bar stays at the top of the page regardles of how far one scrolls. The only caveat is that this navigation bar is for the main webpage "Burke Museum" instead of the section of the page I was on "All About Frogs." That part of the page navigation was only accessible to the top of the page and didn't follow the user when scrolling.

How is the engagement? Is it pleasant to use and appropriate for its industry/topic?

The information is simple and to the point, the headers are more common questions people ask about frogs rather than facts or important aspects to learn about them. It's more surface level rather than more indepth topics, for a page titled "All About Frogs" it doesn't exactly deliver in terms of fact.

Make at least one recommendation to improve this website based on what you learned in this module.

Make the website more accessible for people who are disabled like labeling graphics and giving descriptions to images. Make the information more indepth instead of making the headers of each paragragh questions people commonly ask if they had zero knowledge about the animal to begin with. The topics coveredf could have been done in one or two paragraphs, and the rest facts about their diet, habitat, anatomy, species count, ect.
